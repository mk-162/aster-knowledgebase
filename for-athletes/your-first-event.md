# Your first event: a walkthrough

New to being tracked? Here's the whole journey in order. Each step links to more
detail if you want it.

## Before the day

1. **[Install the app](../getting-started/the-app-and-the-website.md)** (iOS or
   Android). You need the app to be tracked — the website is for watching.
2. **[Create an account](../getting-started/create-an-account.md)** and sign in.
3. **[Register for the event](register-for-an-event.md)** — open its page, tap
   **Join**, fill in the form (race name, bib, category).
4. **Open the event once while you have signal.** This downloads the route and
   caches your registration so you can start later even with no signal.
5. **Allow location and notifications** when the app asks, so tracking and alerts
   work.
6. *(Optional)* **Set emergency contacts** on your profile, and send your
   **[watch link](../for-spectators/share-links.md)** to friends who want to
   follow you.

## At the start line

1. Open the app; your event shows a **countdown**.
2. When the start window opens, tap **Start [event]**.
3. Pick a **[battery mode](battery-modes.md)** to match how long you'll be out.
4. Let the **GPS warm up** (a few seconds) for an accurate first position; tap
   **Start anyway** once it looks good.
5. For a **mass-start race**, you'll see **Tracking armed** until the gun — then
   **GO!**. For a **time trial or group ride**, your clock starts as you begin.

> You can do all of this with **no phone signal** — the app uses your saved copy
> of the event.

## While you're riding

- Watch your live **distance, speed and time** on the ride screen.
- **[Pause](pausing-and-resuming.md)** for a café or mechanical so it doesn't
  count against your moving time; **Resume** to carry on.
- Tap **I'm OK** to reassure watchers, or **SOS** in an emergency —
  [SOS doesn't end your ride](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md).
- Lost signal? Keep riding. Your dot pauses for watchers and **catches up** when
  you reconnect. [More on offline behaviour](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md)

## Finishing

- **Cross the finish line** and you're finished automatically, or tap **Finish**
  and confirm with the short code.
- You'll see your **summary** — time, distance, average speed, and lap splits if
  it was a multi-lap event. [More on finishing](finishing-a-ride.md)

## Afterwards

- Find your result on the **leaderboard** and the event's **results** page.
- Your completed events and stats show on your
  **[profile](results-history-and-profile.md)**.

## Quick reminders

- The app **won't stop your ride or log you out** on its own — you're in control.
- Your numbers are measured strictly between your start and finish, so they're
  consistent everywhere. [Why your numbers are accurate](../safety-and-offline/data-accuracy.md)
