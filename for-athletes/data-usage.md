# Mobile data, battery and what runs offline

A common worry before a big day out: will tracking burn through my data or
battery? Here's what to expect.

## Data: tracking is light

- The biggest one-off download is the **event route and map**, which happens when
  you register or first open the event. Do that on **Wi-Fi** beforehand and it's
  cached for the day.
- While riding, the app sends your position as small updates — light on data.
- If you have **no signal**, tracking uses **no data at all** — your points are
  saved on the phone and uploaded later when you reconnect. See
  [What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).

So a typical ride uses very little data beyond that first route download.

## Watching uses more than riding

If you're **watching** others live (rather than being tracked), that streams a
live map and updates continuously, which uses more data than being tracked does —
similar to using any live map app. On the move, prefer Wi-Fi for long watching
sessions.

## Battery

You choose a [battery mode](battery-modes.md) to match your ride length, and the
app can automatically drop to a more economical mode if your battery runs low.
For long days, pick **Endurance** or **Ultra** and start with a good charge. See
[Tips for a reliable track](tips-for-a-reliable-track.md).

## What works with no signal

Almost everything you need on the day:

- Starting and finishing a ride
- Recording your full route
- Pausing and resuming
- The offline map and your route
- Crash detection and SOS capture

Only **signing up**, your **first** event download, and **live updates to
watchers** need a connection. Everything inside your ride is kept on the phone and
syncs when you're back in range.
