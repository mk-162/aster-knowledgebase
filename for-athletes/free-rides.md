# Just Ride

A **Just Ride** is personal tracking outside any event — a training ride, a run,
a solo adventure. There's no registration, no organiser and no leaderboard. Just
you, your route, and anyone you choose to share it with. Recording a Just Ride is
free, always.

## Start a Just Ride

1. Open the **Go Live** tab. You'll see a big **GO · Ride** (or **GO · Run**)
   button, with three compact chips under it:
   - **activity type** — a **Ride** / **Run** toggle,
   - **who can watch** — defaults to **Just me**,
   - **[battery mode](battery-modes.md)** — defaults to **Standard**.
2. Set the chips how you like them, then tap **GO** to start.
3. Let the short **GPS warm-up** finish so your first position is accurate —
   you're away once the fix looks good.
4. You're tracking. The screen shows your live map, your trail, and your stats —
   distance, elapsed time and current speed.

Like events, a Just Ride **starts fully offline** — no signal required. Your
positions are stored on the phone and upload when you reconnect. See
[What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).

## Share your ride live

Sharing is the **who-can-watch** control — **Who can watch you live**. Choose an
audience:

- **Just me** — nobody else can watch. This is the default, and choosing it
  again stops any sharing you'd turned on.
- **Followers & club** — the people who follow you and your club can watch.
- **Anyone with link** — anyone you hand the link to can watch.

Choosing **Followers & club** or **Anyone with link** creates **one live watch
link**. Share it through your phone's native share sheet or **Copy** it. Whoever
opens it sees your live position, trail, distance and time — **no account, no
app, no login**. There's no limit on how many people can watch, and you don't
need a separate button to switch sharing off — just choose **Just me** again.

You can set this before you start with the **who can watch** chip, and change it
mid-ride from the **Who can watch** pill on the ride screen. A **Share** / **Invite**
button copies or sends the link.

If you turn sharing on while offline, watchers can follow along as soon as you're
back in signal — the app tells you so.

**Watching versus following.** Anyone can *watch* your ride from the link with no
account. To *follow* it — so they get a notification if you send an SOS or when
you finish — a watcher signs in with an Aster account first.

## Pause and resume

A Just Ride pauses and resumes just like an event — your clock and distance hold
while you're stopped. See [Pausing and resuming](pausing-and-resuming.md).

## Safety on a Just Ride

A Just Ride carries two safety tools:

- An **SOS** button (**SOS — Alert my watchers**) that alerts the people watching
  your ride with your location.
- Automatic **crash detection**, which is a Just-Ride-only feature. If your speed
  drops from fast to a dead stop, the app starts a countdown and, if you don't
  cancel, raises a crash SOS to your watchers.

Both alert the people watching you — they do **not** contact the emergency
services, and if nobody's watching, the app tells you plainly. Neither ever ends
your ride. See
[SOS and crash detection](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md) for
exactly who gets alerted and what to do.

## End and save

To finish, use the **slide-to-end** control on the ride screen — *slide to end
your ride*. Your ride saves to your history with its distance, time, average
speed and trail, and you get a link to view it. It ends only when you slide to
end — the app never ends a Just Ride on its own.

If you end while offline, the app records the exact stop moment and uploads the
ride when signal returns — your trail ends where you actually stopped, not where
you reconnected.

## Find your saved rides

Your Just Rides live in **My rides** (and on your profile). Open one to see the
full route and stats, replay it, download the raw GPS track, and choose whether
it's public or private. See
[Your results, history and profile](results-history-and-profile.md).
