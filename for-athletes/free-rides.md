# Free rides

A **free ride** is personal tracking outside any event — a training ride, a run,
a solo adventure. There's no registration, no organiser and no leaderboard. Just
you, your route, and anyone you choose to share it with. Free rides are free,
always.

## Start a free ride

1. On the **Track** screen, tap **Start Free Ride**.
2. Pick a [battery mode](battery-modes.md) to match how long you'll be out.
3. Let the short **GPS warm-up** finish so your first position is accurate, or
   tap **Start anyway** once it looks good.
4. You're tracking. The screen shows your live map, your trail, and your stats —
   distance, elapsed time and current speed.

Like events, free rides **start fully offline** — no signal required. Your
positions are stored on the phone and upload when you reconnect. See
[What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).

## Share your ride live

1. In the **Share Your Ride** section, tap **Generate share link**.
2. You get a **link** you can **Copy** or **Share** through your phone's share
   sheet.
3. Whoever opens it sees your live position, trail, distance and time — **no
   account, no app, no login**.

A free ride has **one active share link at a time** — sharing it with more people
just means handing the same link around; there's no limit on how many people can
watch. If you'd rather retire a link (say you shared it too widely), tap
**Deactivate** and generate a fresh one.

If you generate the link offline, watchers can follow along as soon as you're
back in signal — the app tells you so.

**Watching versus following.** Anyone can *watch* your ride from the link with no
account. To *follow* it — so they get a notification if you send an SOS or when
you finish — a watcher signs in with an Aster account first.

## Pause and resume

Free rides pause and resume just like events — your clock and distance hold while
you're stopped. See [Pausing and resuming](pausing-and-resuming.md).

## Safety on a free ride

Free rides carry two safety tools:

- An **SOS** button (**SOS — Alert my watchers**) that alerts the people watching
  your ride with your location.
- Automatic **crash detection**, which is a free-ride-only feature. If your speed
  drops from fast to a dead stop, the app starts a countdown and, if you don't
  cancel, raises a crash SOS to your watchers.

Both alert the people watching you — they do **not** contact the emergency
services, and if nobody's watching, the app tells you plainly. See
[SOS and crash detection](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md) for
exactly who gets alerted and what to do.

## End and save

Tap **Stop & save ride**. Your ride saves to your history with its distance, time,
average speed and trail, and you get a link to view it. Stopping only happens when
you tap Stop — the app never ends a free ride on its own.

If you stop while offline, the app records the exact stop moment and uploads the
ride when signal returns — your trail ends where you actually stopped, not where
you reconnected.

## Find your saved rides

Your free rides live in **My rides** (and on your profile). Open one to see the
full route and stats, replay it, download the raw GPS track, and choose whether
it's public or private. See
[Your results, history and profile](results-history-and-profile.md).
