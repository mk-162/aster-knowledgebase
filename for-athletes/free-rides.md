# Free rides

A **free ride** is personal tracking outside of any event. There's no
registration and no leaderboard — just you, your route, and anyone you choose to
share it with.

## Start a free ride

1. Tap **Start Free Ride**.
2. Pick a [battery mode](battery-modes.md).
3. After the short GPS warm-up, tap **Start anyway** when you're ready.
4. You're tracking. The screen shows your live map, trail and stats (distance,
   elapsed time, speed, average speed).

Like events, free rides **start fully offline** — no signal required.

## Share your ride live

1. On the ride screen tap **Share your ride**.
2. You'll get a **link** (and a QR code) you can send to anyone.
3. Whoever opens it sees your live position, trail, distance and time — **no
   account needed**.

People watching can choose to **follow** the ride (this needs an account) so they
get a notification if you send an SOS or when you finish.

## Pause and resume

Free rides pause and resume just like events — your clock and distance hold while
you're stopped. See [Pausing and resuming](pausing-and-resuming.md).

## Safety on a free ride

Free rides have the same safety tools as events: an **SOS** button and automatic
**crash detection**. If you crash, the app can raise the alarm and share your
location with the people watching your ride. See
[SOS and crash detection](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md).

## End and save

Tap **Stop & save ride**. Your ride is saved to your history with its distance,
time and average speed, and your trail. You'll get a confirmation with a link to
view it.

If you stop while offline, the app records the exact stop moment and uploads the
ride when signal returns — your trail ends where you actually stopped.

## Find your saved rides

Your free rides live in **My Rides** (and on your profile). Open one to see the
full route and stats, and to download the raw GPS track. See
[Your results, history and profile](results-history-and-profile.md).
