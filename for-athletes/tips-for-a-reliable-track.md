# Tips for a reliable track

Aster is built to keep recording through dead zones, backgrounding and a dying
battery — and to pick tracking back up if your phone's operating system tries to
shut it down. A few habits make your track even cleaner and head off day-of
surprises.

## Before you go

- **Allow location "all the time".** Tracking has to keep recording while the app
  is in your pocket with the screen off. When the app asks for location, choose
  the **always / allow all the time** option, not "only while using the app".
- **Allow notifications.** These power your start reminders, the persistent
  tracking notification, and safety alerts.
- **Let the app run in the background.** Some Android phones (Samsung, Xiaomi,
  OnePlus, Huawei and others) run aggressive battery savers that shut background
  apps down. Exclude Aster from **battery optimisation** so your tracking isn't
  killed mid-ride. Aster will restart tracking by itself when you next open it,
  but excluding it up front avoids gaps in the first place.
- **Register and open the event once with signal.** That downloads the route and
  caches everything so you can start offline later. See
  [Register for an event](register-for-an-event.md). (A free ride needs no
  download — just start it.)
- **Start with a good charge** and pick the right
  [battery mode](battery-modes.md) for how long you'll be out.

## At the start

- **Let the GPS warm up.** Step into the open, away from buildings and tree cover,
  and let the short warm-up finish so your first position is accurate. Tap **Start
  anyway** once it looks good rather than starting against a wall.
- **Give your phone a clear view of the sky** where you can. A phone on the bars
  or an arm strap gets a cleaner signal than one buried deep in a bag or pocket.

## While you're out

- **Don't force-quit the app.** Leave it running in the background — it keeps
  recording. Swiping it away can stop tracking.
- **Lost signal? Keep going.** Your points are saved on the phone and upload when
  you reconnect; your dot catches up for watchers. See
  [What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).
- **Use Pause for real stops** so a café, a mechanical or a nature break doesn't
  count against your moving time. See [Pausing and resuming](pausing-and-resuming.md).

## After

- **Open the app with signal once you're done**, especially if you were in a dead
  zone, so any saved points finish uploading and your track fills in completely.

## If something looks off

- A **short track or a missing section** after an offline ride usually fills in
  once the app uploads its saved points — open it with signal and give it a moment.
- Your **finish time is always safe**, even if the very tail of the trail is still
  uploading. See
  [Why your distance and time are accurate](../safety-and-offline/data-accuracy.md).
