# Tips for a reliable track

The app is built to keep recording through dead zones, backgrounding and low
battery. A few habits make your track even cleaner and avoid day-of surprises.

## Before you ride

- **Allow location in the background.** Tracking needs to keep recording while the
  app is in your pocket and the screen is off. When the app asks for location,
  grant the "always / allow all the time" option, not "only while using".
- **Allow notifications.** This powers reminders, the tracking notification, and
  safety alerts.
- **Let the app run in the background.** On some Android phones, aggressive
  battery savers can shut apps down — exclude Aster from battery
  optimisation so background tracking isn't killed.
- **Register and open the event once with signal.** That downloads the route and
  caches everything so you can start offline later. See
  [Register for an event](register-for-an-event.md).
- **Start with a good charge** and pick the right
  [battery mode](battery-modes.md) for how long you'll be out.

## At the start

- **Let the GPS warm up.** Step into the open, away from buildings and trees, and
  let the short warm-up finish so your first position is accurate. Tap **Start
  anyway** once it looks good rather than starting against a building.
- **Mount your phone with a clear view of the sky** where you can. A phone deep in
  a bag or jersey pocket gets a slightly noisier signal than one on the bars.

## During the ride

- **Don't force-quit the app.** Leave it running in the background — it keeps
  recording. Swiping it away can stop tracking.
- **Lost signal? Keep going.** Your points are saved on the phone and upload when
  you reconnect; your dot catches up for watchers. See
  [What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).
- **Use Pause for real stops** so a café or mechanical doesn't count against your
  moving time. See [Pausing and resuming](pausing-and-resuming.md).

## After

- **Open the app with signal once you finish**, especially if you were in a dead
  zone, so any saved points finish uploading and your track fills in completely.

## If something looks off

- A **short track or a missing section** after an offline ride usually fills in
  once the app uploads its saved points — open it with signal.
- Your **finish time is always safe**, even if the very tail of the trail is
  missing — see [Why your distance and time are accurate](../safety-and-offline/data-accuracy.md).
