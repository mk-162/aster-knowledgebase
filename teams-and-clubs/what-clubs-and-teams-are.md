# What clubs and teams are on Aster

A **team** on Aster is any group of athletes under one name — a cycling club, a
running club, a group of mates, a race squad. "Club" and "team" are the same
thing in the app; a club is simply a team, with or without a paid plan.

Every team gets a page of its own with a roster, a rides tab, a club board, a
route library, and a record of the events its members have taken part in.
Anyone can create one, free.

## Free groups vs the Club plan

A free team is perfect for a group of mates: up to **10 active members**, who
can schedule one-off members-only group rides together. A paid **Club plan**
removes the member limit and adds the tools a real club needs week in, week
out.

| What you get | Free team | Club plan |
|---|---|---|
| Team page, roster, follows | Yes | Yes |
| Members | Up to 10 active members | Unlimited |
| Members-only group rides | One-off rides | Unlimited, including weekly repeating rides |
| Club board (members-only posts) | Yes | Yes |
| Club route library | Yes | Yes |
| RSVP list, attendance register and CSV export | Yes | Yes |
| "Everyone home" view for ride organisers | Yes | Yes |
| Club sponsor slot on your pages | — | Yes |
| Members follow unlimited favourites | — (5-favourite limit applies) | Yes |

Plans are priced by club size and billed monthly or annually by card. Exact
prices are on [astertrack.app/pricing](https://astertrack.app/pricing).

**Good to know:** taking part in events and spectating are free for everyone,
always. The Club plan pays for club tools — it never gates riding, running or
watching.

## What stays free for everyone

- **Spectating.** Anyone can watch a public event or a shared ride link
  without an account — see [share links](../for-spectators/share-links.md).
- **Taking part.** Registering for events, tracking rides and runs, results —
  all free.
- **Being in a team.** Joining a team costs the member nothing, whether or not
  the team has a plan.

## Where to find teams

In the app, open **Teams** to browse and search, or follow a link straight to
a club's page. Each team page shows its logo, description, member roster and
tabs for **Rides**, **Feed** (the club board), **Routes** and **Events**.
