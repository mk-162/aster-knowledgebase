# Share links: events and shared rides

A link is all anyone needs to watch on Aster. There's nothing to install and no
account to create.

## Event links

Every event has its own web address based on its name (for example
`…/events/dirty-reiver-2026`). Open it and you get the event page — details,
route, and (when it's live) the map, trails and rider list.

- **Public** and **unlisted** event links work for anyone, no account needed.
- **Private** and **members-only** event links require signing in, and are
  visible only to people in the event or club. See
  [privacy levels](public-unlisted-private-events.md).

These links are perfect to post on social media or print on a poster.

## Ride watch links

When someone wants to share a [Just Ride](../for-athletes/free-rides.md), they
choose who can watch from the **"Who can watch you live"** control on the ride:
**Just me**, **Followers & club**, or **Anyone with link**. Picking one of the
sharing options creates a single **live watch link** (it looks like
`…/ride/watch/…`), which they pass on through the share sheet or by tapping
Copy. Choosing **Just me** stops sharing. Whoever opens the link sees the
rider's live location, trail, distance and time — with no account required.

- The link is long, random and unguessable — only people the rider sends it to
  can find the ride.
- There's **one live link per ride**, tied to the audience control. There's no
  separate button to deactivate a link or generate a fresh one: sharing is on
  while the audience is **Followers & club** or **Anyone with link**, and
  switching to **Just me** turns it off.
- If the rider pauses, the watch page says so — the marker holds position and
  the distance and time freeze until they resume.
- After the ride ends, the link still opens, but instead of live updates it
  shows a **finished-ride summary** — the distance covered and how long it took.
- If the rider raises an **SOS**, everyone with the watch page open sees the
  alert with their location. To also get a push notification when you're *not*
  looking at the page, you need to be signed in — see
  [Following riders and getting notified](following-and-notifications.md).

## Good to know

- Sharing a link never exposes anything beyond the ride itself — a watcher
  sees the live ride, not the rider's account details or ride history.
- Watching by link is never limited or counted — open as many shared rides as
  you like, with or without an account.
