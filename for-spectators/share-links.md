# Share links: events and free rides

## Event links

Every event has its own web address based on its name (for example
`…/events/dirty-reiver-2026`). Open it and you get the event page — details,
route, and (when it's live) the map, trails and leaderboard.

- **Public** and **unlisted** event links work for anyone, no account needed.
- **Private** event links require signing in and the organiser's access code.

These links are perfect to post on social media or print on a poster.

## Free-ride links

When a rider shares a free ride, they generate a **watch link** (and a QR code).
Whoever opens it sees the rider's live position, trail, distance and time — with
no account required.

- The link is private and unguessable — only people the rider sends it to can
  find the ride.
- After the ride ends, the link still opens and shows the finished ride; it just
  stops updating live.
- Opening a ride lets you watch; to get notified (e.g. an SOS or finish) you can
  **follow** it, which needs an account.

## Good to know

- Sharing a link never exposes anything private — a watcher sees the live ride,
  not the rider's account details.
- If an organiser changes an event from public to private after sharing, old
  links will stop working for people without access.
