# Share links: events and shared rides

A link is all anyone needs to watch on Aster. There's nothing to install and no
account to create.

## Event links

Every event has its own web address based on its name (for example
`…/events/dirty-reiver-2026`). Open it and you get the event page — details,
route, and (when it's live) the map, trails and rider list.

- **Public** and **unlisted** event links work for anyone, no account needed.
- **Private** and **members-only** event links require signing in, and are
  visible only to people in the event or club. See
  [privacy levels](public-unlisted-private-events.md).

These links are perfect to post on social media or print on a poster.

## Ride watch links

When someone shares a [free ride](../for-athletes/free-rides.md), they generate
a **watch link** (with a QR code to scan). It looks like `…/ride/watch/…`.
Whoever opens it sees the rider's live position, trail, distance and time —
with no account required.

- The link is long, random and unguessable — only people the rider sends it to
  can find the ride.
- Each ride has one active link at a time. The rider can turn a link off from
  their ride screen and generate a fresh one; a link that's been turned off
  tells new visitors it's no longer active.
- If the rider pauses, the watch page says so — the dot holds position and the
  distance and time freeze until they resume.
- After the ride ends, the link still opens and shows the finished ride; it
  just stops updating live.
- If the rider raises an **SOS**, everyone with the watch page open sees the
  alert with their location. To also get a push notification when you're *not*
  looking at the page, you need to be signed in — see
  [Following riders and getting notified](following-and-notifications.md).

## Good to know

- Sharing a link never exposes anything beyond the ride itself — a watcher
  sees the live ride, not the rider's account details or ride history.
- Watching by link is never limited or counted — it doesn't touch the
  favourites cap that applies to following athletes with an account.
