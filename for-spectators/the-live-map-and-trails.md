# Reading the live map and trails

## Dots

Each rider is a coloured **dot** at their latest known position. Dots move in
near real time as riders send their location. Tap a dot (or a rider in the list)
to focus on that rider.

## Trails

A **trail** is the line showing where a rider has been. It's drawn from the GPS
points the rider has recorded, and it grows as they ride.

### Why trails sometimes have gaps

If a rider loses phone signal, their points can't upload until they reconnect, so
the trail shows a **break** and then resumes. A gap simply means "no live data
through here" — usually a dead zone. When the rider's phone catches up, the
missing section fills in.

A break also appears wherever a rider **paused** — that's an accurate picture of
where they stopped.

## Live updates

- Positions stream live while you have the page open. If your own connection
  drops, the map reconnects and resumes updating.
- On a long ride, the trail you see is the **recent** history; the full track is
  always preserved in the rider's saved ride.

## Finished riders freeze

The moment a rider finishes, their dot **stops at the finish line** and stays
there. Even if their phone keeps sending points afterwards (driving home, for
example), those are ignored — a finished rider never drifts on the map. See
[Why your distance and time are accurate](../safety-and-offline/data-accuracy.md).
