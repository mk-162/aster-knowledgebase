# Watch an event live

Following a race on Aster is meant to be effortless. For **public** and
**unlisted** events you don't need an account, an app, or any setup — just open
the link.

## How to watch

1. Open the event link (shared with you, or found in **Discover**). It works in
   any browser, including a private/incognito window.
2. The event page loads with the **live map**: every active rider's dot, the
   route, and the trails riders have left behind.
3. Switch to the **Riders** tab to see the live **leaderboard** — standings,
   gaps, and pace.
4. Tap any rider to focus on them and see their trail and stats.

No sign-in prompt will appear. If you ever hit one while watching a public event,
that's a bug — watching is meant to be open.

## What you can see without an account

- The live map and every rider's dot
- Each rider's trail (the path they've ridden)
- The route line and the finish
- The live leaderboard and gaps
- Checkpoints / segments where the event uses them
- The route file (GPX) to download

See the detail in [Reading the live map and trails](the-live-map-and-trails.md)
and [Reading the leaderboard](the-leaderboard.md).

## What needs an account

Anything that's tied to *you* needs signing in:

- **Following** an event or rider to get notifications
- Posting, commenting or reacting

See [Following riders and getting notified](following-and-notifications.md).

## Private events

Private events are the exception: they require signing in (and the organiser's
access code) to view. See
[Public, unlisted and private events](public-unlisted-private-events.md).

## Good to know

- Positions update **live** as riders send them. If a rider is in a no-signal
  area, their dot pauses and then catches up when they reconnect.
- When a rider **finishes**, their dot stops at the line and their result locks
  in — it won't drift around afterwards.
