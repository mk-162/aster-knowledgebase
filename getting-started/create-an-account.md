# Create an account

## Do I even need one?

Not always. **Watching** public and unlisted events — and any ride someone
shares with you by link — works with no account at all.

You need an account when you want to **do** something that's tied to you:

- Register for an event and be tracked as a rider.
- Record your own free rides.
- Follow riders, teams or events and get notifications.
- Post updates, comment, or react.
- Create or manage an event as an organiser.

## Sign up

1. Open the app (or the website) and choose **Sign Up**.
2. Enter your **display name** (this is the name shown on leaderboards and your
   profile), your **email**, and a **password** (at least 8 characters).
3. Confirm — your account is created and you're signed in.

You'll receive a short series of welcome emails over your first week introducing
the main things you can do.

## Sign in

Enter your email and password. Once you're signed in, you **stay** signed in —
the app does not log you out automatically, even if the network drops or an error
occurs mid-ride. This is deliberate: being logged out in the middle of nowhere
would be a real problem. See
[What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md)

The only times your session ends are when **you** tap Sign Out, or if the app
detects its saved login is corrupted on your device.

## Forgotten password

On the sign-in screen choose **Forgot password**, enter your email, and we'll
send a reset link. The link is valid for a limited time; open it and choose a new
password. For security we always say "if that email exists, a link has been sent"
— so you won't be told whether an address is registered.

For changing your password or email **while signed in**, see
[Account and signing in](../account-and-privacy/account-and-signing-in.md).

## Good to know

- **One account, all roles.** The same account works as a rider, a spectator, an
  organiser and a team member. You don't need separate logins.
- **The app keeps your data on your device.** Your login and any ride in progress
  are stored on the phone so the app works offline. They stay there until you
  explicitly sign out.
