# Key concepts

A few core ideas show up everywhere in Aster. Understanding these makes
everything else straightforward.

## The dot

Your **dot** is your live position on the map. As you ride, the dot moves and
leaves a **trail** (the line showing where you've been). Spectators watch your
dot and trail update in near real time.

## Pings

A **ping** is a single GPS point — your location at one moment. The app records
pings as you ride and sends them to Aster, which connects them into your
trail and adds up the distance between them. If you lose signal, pings are saved
on your phone and sent later; nothing inside your ride is lost.

## Events vs free rides

There are two ways to be tracked:

| | **Event** | **Free ride** |
|---|---|---|
| What it is | An organised race, group ride or adventure you register for | A personal ride you track on your own |
| Set up by | An event organiser | You, on the spot |
| Who's on the map | All registered riders | Just you |
| Has a leaderboard | Yes | No |
| Who can watch | Spectators of that event | Anyone you share your ride link with |

See [Register for an event](../for-athletes/register-for-an-event.md) and
[Free rides](../for-athletes/free-rides.md).

## Event types

Events come in a few flavours, which affect how timing and finishing work:

- **Mass-start race** — everyone starts together on a "gun"; first across the
  line wins.
- **Time trial** — riders start one at a time; each is timed on their own clock.
- **Group ride / adventure (point-to-point)** — social or solo, started
  individually, often without competitive timing.

See [Event types explained](../for-organisers/event-types.md).

## Start moment and finish moment

Every ride is bounded by two moments: when it **started** and when it **ended**.
Everything shown about your ride — your trail, distance and time — is measured
strictly between those two moments. Points recorded before your start or after
your finish are ignored. This is what keeps your numbers honest, and it's why a
finished rider's dot stops dead at the line. See
[Why your distance and time are accurate](../safety-and-offline/data-accuracy.md).

## Privacy levels (events)

- **Public** — listed in discovery and watchable by anyone with the link.
- **Unlisted** — not listed anywhere, but watchable by anyone who has the link.
- **Private** — requires signing in (and an access code) to view.

See [Public, unlisted and private events](../for-spectators/public-unlisted-private-events.md).

## Handles

A **handle** is your @username (for example `@dirtyreiver`). People, teams,
events and organisers can all have one. Handles make profiles easy to link to and
let you **@mention** someone in a post or comment.
