# Push notifications and devices

## How push works

When you allow notifications, your device registers to receive **push** from
Aster. You can be registered on more than one device — your phone and a browser,
say — and you'll get push on each.

Push is most reliable in the **mobile app** (Android and iPhone). Some browsers
can show web push too, but the app is the dependable option — see
[the app and the website](../getting-started/the-app-and-the-website.md).

## Turning push on

1. Allow notifications for Aster when your phone or browser asks. If you dismissed
   that prompt, enable notifications for Aster in your device's system settings.
2. In the app, keep the master **Push notifications** switch on (see
   [Preferences and quiet hours](preferences-and-quiet-hours.md)).

That's it — there's no device list to manage. Each device you sign in on and
allow notifications for is registered automatically.

## If push stops working

The Notification settings screen watches for the common failure and tells you
about it right there: if your phone is **blocking** notifications, or this device
**never finished registering**, you'll see a short warning with a **Try again**
button. Tapping it re-attempts registration.

{% include screenshot-todo.html slug="notifications-push-blocked-banner" caption="The Notification settings warning that appears when the OS is blocking push or the device isn't registered, with the Try again button." %}

If nothing is arriving:

- Check your device's **system notification settings** for Aster — push can be
  blocked there even when the app's own switch is on.
- Confirm the master **Push notifications** switch is on and the specific type
  isn't muted.
- Check you're not inside your **quiet hours** window.
- Reopen the app, or tap **Try again** on the settings screen, to refresh this
  device's registration.

> **Good to know:** even with push off or blocked, your **in-app alerts list**
> still records everything, so you can always catch up there.
