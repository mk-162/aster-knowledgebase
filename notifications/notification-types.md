# Notification types

Aster notifies you about the athletes, teams and events you follow, and about
activity on your own account. You choose which of these you receive — see
[Preferences and quiet hours](preferences-and-quiet-hours.md).

Notifications are **nudges, not commands**. Nothing you receive — and nothing
in the tray — can start or stop someone's ride. That's always the rider's
decision.

## Watching an athlete or a race

These go to the people **following an athlete or event** — spectators, teammates
and fans.

- **Race progress** — an athlete you follow signs up for or starts a race, and
  as they go: lap splits and distance milestones (for example passing a round
  distance or reaching a checkpoint on a point-to-point route).
- **Race finishes and cutoffs** — a rider you're watching crosses the line, hits
  the organiser's cutoff time, or doesn't finish.
- **Stoppages and off-route** — a tracked rider hasn't moved for a while, or has
  strayed from the course.
- **Feed-zone approaches** — during a race, a countdown as a rider you're
  watching nears a feed station.
- **Gone quiet** — when you're watching someone's Just Ride, an alert if their
  tracking hasn't updated for a while, so you know before you start worrying.

> **Good to know:** ride or run, these updates only ever describe what a rider is
> doing. A "stopped" alert means "still out there, not moving" — someone fixing a
> puncture or waiting out a shower is still on their ride. It never means the ride
> has ended.

**Sportives and other untimed formats show times, not positions.** For a sportive
gran fondo — and for group rides and grand departs — there are no rankings, so
nothing you receive ever talks about positions or places. Progress updates stick
to distance, milestones and checkpoints, which suit every format. See
[event types](../for-organisers/event-types.md).

## Events you follow

- **Event reminders and announcements** — the countdown to a race you've
  registered for, and posts from the organiser of an event you ride or follow.
- **New events from organisers you follow** — someone whose events you follow has
  published a new one.
- **An event you starred is live** — an event you starred has just started, so you
  can tap straight in to watch.

## Your own account

These go to **you**, about your own posts, follows and messages.

- **New follower** — someone followed you.
- **Likes on your posts** — someone liked a post or story of yours.
- **Comments on your posts** — someone commented on your post or story, or replied
  to your comment.
- **Mentions** — someone @mentioned you in a post, story or comment.
- **Messages from an organiser** — an organiser sent you a message during a
  [safety alert](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md), or broadcast an
  announcement to everyone in an event you're riding.

> **Good to know:** Aster doesn't have a general inbox or direct messaging between
> riders. "Messages" here means organiser-to-rider messages — an announcement to
> a whole event, or the two-way chat that opens during an SOS.

## Teams

- **Team updates** — your request to join a team was received, approved or
  declined, or you were made the owner of a team.

## Safety

- **SOS** — a rider you're watching has raised an emergency alert (or crash
  detection raised one for them). These always come through, even during quiet
  hours. An SOS alerts the people watching that ride — it does **not** contact
  the emergency services. See [SOS and safety](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md).

## Where notifications appear

- As **push notifications** on your phone (see
  [Push notifications and devices](push-and-devices.md)).
- In your **in-app alerts list** (the bell), which records everything even when
  push is off — so you never silently miss something.

Choose which types reach you in
[Preferences and quiet hours](preferences-and-quiet-hours.md).
