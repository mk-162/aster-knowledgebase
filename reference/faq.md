# Frequently asked questions

The general FAQ — what Aster is, how it compares with other trackers, and how
organisers get set up — lives on the website:
[astertrack.app/faq](https://astertrack.app/faq). This page keeps the questions
that come up in support.

## Cost and accounts

**Does Aster cost anything?**
Not for you as an athlete or a watcher — taking part in events and spectating
are free, and stay free. Paid plans exist for the people running things: club
plans (priced by member count — a small group of mates is free), an event
organiser subscription that includes a number of events per month, and an
enterprise tier by arrangement. Current prices are on
[astertrack.app/pricing](https://astertrack.app/pricing).

**Do I need an account to watch?**
No. Public and unlisted events, and any ride shared with you by link, are
watchable in a browser with no account and no app install. Only private and
members-only events require signing in. See
[Watching an event](../for-spectators/index.md).

**Why can't I follow more athletes?**
Free accounts can follow up to 5 athletes as favourites; membership of a
subscribed club removes the cap. The cap never affects watching — anyone can
open a shared link or a public event page.

## Safety

**Does SOS call the emergency services?**
No. An SOS alerts the people watching your ride and the event organiser with
your live location — it does not contact the emergency services, and nobody at
Aster is monitoring your ride. The SOS screen offers a one-tap call to 999, and
if nobody is watching, the app tells you so plainly. Treat it as a way to alert
your people, not as a rescue service. See
[SOS and crash detection](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md).

## Offline and signal

**Can I start a ride with no phone signal?**
Yes. Starting and finishing both work fully offline; your points are saved on
the phone and upload when signal returns. See
[What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).

**My ride saved short, or a section is missing.**
If you were offline, the missing part usually fills in once your phone uploads
its saved points — open the app somewhere with signal and let it catch up.
Don't reinstall the app: unsent ride data is stored on the device.

**My battery died mid-ride — did I lose my ride?**
No. Everything recorded so far is saved on the phone. Charge up, reopen the
app, and it uploads what's queued.

## Battery

**Will the app drain my battery?**
Tracking all day costs battery — Aster is honest about that. You choose a
battery mode to match your ride length, and the app can drop to a more
economical mode when your battery runs low. See
[Battery modes](../for-athletes/battery-modes.md) and the website's
[battery guide](https://astertrack.app/battery-use).

**Do I need to keep the screen on or the app open?**
No. Leave the app in the background with the screen off — it keeps recording.
Just don't force-quit (swipe away) the app mid-ride. See
[Tips for a reliable track](../for-athletes/tips-for-a-reliable-track.md).

## Riding and results

**Will the app ever stop my ride or log me out by itself?**
No. Your ride continues until you end it, you cross the event's finish line, or
the organiser's cut-off passes — and no server or network error ever signs you
out mid-ride. If your session needs refreshing you'll see a banner; your ride
carries on regardless.

**Why doesn't my race time start when I tapped Start?**
In a mass-start race everyone is timed from the gun, not from the moment they
individually tapped Start. See
[Starting and tracking a ride](../for-athletes/starting-and-tracking-a-ride.md).

**I forgot to stop my free ride and it tracked my drive home.**
Stop it as soon as you notice — tap **Stop & save ride**. For events this isn't
an issue: finishing freezes your ride at the line, and nothing after it counts.

**Can I change my category or withdraw after registering?**
Usually yes, while registration is open — what's allowed, and until when, is
set by the organiser. See
[Manage or cancel your registration](../for-athletes/manage-your-registration.md).

**Does it work with my watch or bike computer?**
Tracking is done by the phone app — there's no separate smartwatch or
head-unit app today.

## Privacy and data

**Who can see my location?**
Only while you're tracking, and only the right people: in an event, whoever can
view that event (per its privacy level); on a free ride, just the people you
share the link with. See
[Who can see your location?](../account-and-privacy/who-can-see-your-location.md).

**Is the app tracking me when I'm not riding?**
No. Your location is only recorded between the moment you start a ride and the
moment it ends.

**Will tracking use a lot of mobile data?**
No. The main download is the route and map data (done when you register — do it
on Wi-Fi if you can); tracking itself is light, and with no signal it uses no
data at all. Watching others live uses more. See
[Mobile data, battery and what runs offline](../for-athletes/data-usage.md).

## Account

**How do I reset my password?**
Use **Forgot password** on the sign-in screen. See
[Account and signing in](../account-and-privacy/account-and-signing-in.md).

**How do I delete my account or download my data?**
Both are in your settings. See
[Export or delete your data](../account-and-privacy/export-or-delete-your-data.md).
