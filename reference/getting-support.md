# Getting help and reporting a problem

Stuck? Here's the fastest route to an answer.

## First, check these

Most questions are answered already:

- **[Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md)** — fixes for the common issues
  (a dot that isn't moving, a ride that hasn't uploaded, sign-in trouble,
  notifications).
- **[Frequently asked questions](faq.md)** — the questions people ask most.
- Your section's guides: [athletes](../for-athletes/index.md),
  [spectators](../for-spectators/index.md),
  [organisers](../for-organisers/index.md),
  [teams and clubs](../teams-and-clubs/index.md).

## Who to ask

- **Questions about a specific event** — registration, your category, start
  times, results — are best answered by **that event's organiser**. They
  control the event's settings.
- **Bugs, problems mid-ride, or anything safety-relevant** — email
  [help@astertrack.app](mailto:help@astertrack.app). Put "URGENT" in the
  subject if it's safety-relevant and it will be triaged the same day.
- **General questions** about free rides, share links, your profile or an
  event — email [hello@astertrack.app](mailto:hello@astertrack.app).
- The full list of contact channels (press, partnerships and so on) is at
  [astertrack.app/contact](https://astertrack.app/contact).

## Reporting a bug well

A good report gets fixed faster. Please include:

- **What you did**, what you **expected**, and what **happened** instead.
- **Which event or ride** it was about (a link helps).
- Your **device and app version** (for example "iPhone 14, app 2.3").
- The **time** it happened (and your time zone), so it can be traced.
- A **screenshot** if you can.

**Important:** don't sign out or reinstall the app to "fix" a problem
mid-ride — your unsent ride data lives on the device, and the app will sort
itself out once you have signal. See [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md)
first.

## If it's an emergency on a ride

Aster's **SOS** alerts the people watching your ride and the event organiser
with your live location — it is **not** a call to the emergency services, and
nobody at Aster is monitoring your ride. In a real emergency, call 999 (the
SOS screen has a one-tap call button). See
[SOS and crash detection](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md).
