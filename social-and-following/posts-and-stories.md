# Posts and stories

Aster has a light social layer so you can share your riding and running — and
follow other people's — without leaving for another app.

## Write a post

A post is text plus media, shared to your followers or the public. To create one,
tap **Write** and:

- Add **photos and GIFs**, or open the **editor** to build a richer image.
- Attach a **ride or run** so the post shows its distance, time and elevation, or
  attach an **event** to tie a recap to a race.
- Choose **who can see it**:
  - **Public** — anyone, signed in or not.
  - **Followers** — only the people who follow you.
  - **Private** — only you (handy for drafts).
- Save it as a **draft** and publish when you're ready.

## The image editor

The editor turns a photo into a shareable image. On top of a base photo you can
lay:

- **Text** — captions and titles, positioned where you want.
- **GIFs and stickers** from GIPHY — these stay **animated** wherever the post is
  seen: in the editor, in the feed and on the post itself.
- **Emoji**.
- **Ride-data stickers** — your distance, time or other stats from a ride, dropped
  straight onto the image.

Drag to move a layer; use two fingers to resize and rotate it. What you build is
exactly what everyone sees — the same live image renders in the feed and on the
post, so animated GIFs never flatten into a still.

## Event feed posts

On an event, organisers — and, where the organiser allows it, participants — can
post to the **event feed**: announcements, updates and recaps that registered
riders and event followers see. Organiser posts can notify the event's audience;
see [Notification types](../notifications/notification-types.md).

## Posting as a team

If you manage a team, you can post **as the team** rather than as yourself — the
post lands on the team's feed and its followers see it.

## Club-only posts

Clubs also have a **members-only board** — a private noticeboard on the club page
that only active members can read or post to, and that never appears in any public
feed. It's the place for "meet at the other car park this week" and ride notices.
See [the club board](../teams-and-clubs/the-club-board.md).

## Who can delete what

- **Your own stories** — only you can delete them.
- **Event feed posts** — the author can delete their own, and the event's
  organiser can remove any post on their event's feed.

## Good to know

- Public posts are visible to everyone, including people without an account.
- Followers-only and private posts respect those limits everywhere they appear.
- See [Comments, reactions and mentions](comments-reactions-mentions.md) for how
  people engage with your posts.
