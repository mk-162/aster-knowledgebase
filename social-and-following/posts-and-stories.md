# Posts and stories

Aster has a light social layer so you can share your riding and follow
others' — without leaving for another app.

## Stories

A **story** is a post you create — text plus media — to share an update or a
recap. You can:

- Add **photos, GIFs and short video**, or build a richer **composition**
  (a base photo with text, animated stickers and your ride stats laid over it).
- Attach a **ride** so your story shows its distance, time and elevation, or
  attach an **event** to tie a recap to a race.
- Choose **who can see it**:
  - **Public** — anyone, signed in or not.
  - **Followers** — only the people who follow you.
  - **Private** — only you (handy for drafts).
- Save it as a **draft** and publish when you're ready.

## Event feed posts

Organisers (and, on some events, participants) can post to an **event feed** —
announcements, updates and recaps that registered riders and event followers see.
Organiser posts can notify the event's audience; see
[Notification types](../notifications/notification-types.md).

## Posting as a team

If you manage a team, you can post **as the team** rather than as yourself — the
post lands on the team feed and the team's followers see it.

## Good to know

- Public stories are visible to everyone, including people without an account.
- Followers-only and private stories respect those limits everywhere they appear.
- See [Comments, reactions and mentions](comments-reactions-mentions.md) for how
  people engage with your posts.
