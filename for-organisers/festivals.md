# Festivals and series (multi-event days)

When you run more than one event under a single banner — a weekend with 200 km,
100 km and 50 km distances, or a one-day gravel day with several distances — you
group them together so riders and spectators see them as one thing. In the app
this grouping is called a **festival**: a day or weekend bundling several events.

Above festivals sits a **series** — a calendar of festivals over a season. It's
the level you use when the same festival returns each year, or when a championship
runs across several festivals.

## Create a festival and add events

1. Create the festival with a name, description and header image.
2. Add your events to it — when you create each event, choose **Part of a
   festival** in the naming step and pick an existing festival or name a new one.
   You can also add an existing event later from the festival's manage screen.
3. The festival gets its own public page listing every event, **sorted by
   distance**, with each event's start time, categories, wave times and **live
   registration counts**. Riders can register for an event and category straight
   from that page.

You manage the festival and its events as their organiser, and you can move an
event in or out of the festival at any time.

## Group festivals into a series

On a festival's manage screen, the **Part of a series?** control links that
festival to a **series** — a calendar of festivals over a season — so spectators
can follow the whole calendar in one place. Add the festival to an existing series
you manage, or create a new series by name; you can remove it again at any time.
The series link is stored on the festival, never on the individual events.

## Share a course across races

If several races run on the same roads (common at a festival), build a **course**
once — its shape, its start/finish lines and its feed-stop locations — and apply
it to every race in one go, with a preview before you commit. Edit the course in
one place and push the changes to all linked races, instead of re-uploading a GPX
for each. A single race can keep its own version of a piece (its own feed stops,
say) if it needs to differ. See
[Routes, start and finish lines](routes-start-and-finish.md).

## Festival-wide drafting check

For a competitive festival, you can run a **drafting scan** across all its events
at once — it catches riders sitting on a wheel even when they're in different
events on shared road, which a single-event check can't see. It surfaces flagged pairs for
you to review and clear, penalise or disqualify. Disqualifying a rider asks you to
confirm first.

## Good to know

- The festival page only shows events a viewer is allowed to see — private events
  stay hidden from anyone who doesn't manage the festival.
- Registration counts and category spots on the page update live as riders sign up.
