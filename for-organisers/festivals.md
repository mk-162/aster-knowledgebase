# Festivals and series (multi-race events)

When you run more than one race under a single banner — a weekend with 200 km,
100 km and 50 km distances, or a round of a championship — you group them together
so riders and spectators see them as one event. In the app this is called a
**series** (you may also see the term **festival** for the group page itself).

## Create a series and add races

1. Create the series with a name, description and header image.
2. Add your races to it — when you create each race, pick the series it belongs to,
   or add an existing race later from the series' manage screen.
3. The series gets its own public page listing every race, **sorted by distance**,
   with each race's start time, categories, wave times and **live registration
   counts**. Riders can register for a race and category straight from that page.

You manage the series and its races as their organiser, and you can move a race in
or out of the series at any time.

## Share a course across races

If several races run on the same roads (common at a festival), build a **course**
once — its shape, its start/finish lines and its feed-stop locations — and apply
it to every race in one go, with a preview before you commit. Edit the course in
one place and push the changes to all linked races, instead of re-uploading a GPX
for each. A single race can keep its own version of a piece (its own feed stops,
say) if it needs to differ. See
[Routes, start and finish lines](routes-start-and-finish.md).

## Festival-wide drafting check

For competitive series, you can run a **drafting scan** across all the races at
once — it catches riders sitting on a wheel even when they're in different races on
shared road, which a single-race check can't see. It surfaces flagged pairs for
you to review and clear, penalise or disqualify. Disqualifying a rider asks you to
confirm first.

## Good to know

- The series page only shows races a viewer is allowed to see — private races stay
  hidden from anyone who doesn't manage the series.
- Registration counts and category spots on the page update live as riders sign up.
