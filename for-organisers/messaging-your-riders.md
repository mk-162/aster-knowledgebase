# Messaging your riders

You have three distinct ways to reach the people in your event, each suited to a
different moment.

## 1. The event feed — announcements and updates

Post to the **event feed** for updates that show on the event page, where
registered riders and event followers see them: schedule notes, course changes,
weather calls, post-event recaps. When you post as the organiser, your event's
followers can be notified — so the feed is your "everyone should know this"
channel that also lives on the page for anyone who arrives later.

Best for: pre-event information, day-of updates, post-event recaps.

## 2. Message riders — a direct push to everyone

**Message riders** sends a push notification (with a title and an optional link)
straight to **all participants** in the event. It's the most direct way to get a
short, important message in front of riders fast. You send it from the event card
on your [dashboard](race-day-management.md).

Best for: time-sensitive alerts — "neutral start delayed 15 minutes", "route
diverted at checkpoint 3".

## 3. SOS chat — talking to one rider in trouble

When a rider raises an **SOS**, you can **message them directly** from the alert,
and they can reply — a two-way thread while you coordinate help. See
[Handling SOS alerts](handling-sos-alerts.md).

Best for: a live safety situation with a specific rider.

## Which to use

| You want to… | Use |
|---|---|
| Share info on the event page (and notify your followers) | **Event feed** |
| Push a short, urgent message to all riders now | **Message riders** |
| Talk to one rider during an SOS | **SOS chat** |

## Good to know

- Riders control their own [notification preferences](../notifications/preferences-and-quiet-hours.md),
  so non-urgent messages may not push to everyone — the in-app feed and
  notification list still capture them.
- Keep the direct **Message riders** push for genuinely time-sensitive messages,
  so riders keep paying attention to it.
- See the rider's side in [Notification types](../notifications/notification-types.md).
