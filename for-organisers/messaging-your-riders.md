# Messaging your riders

You have three distinct ways to communicate, each suited to a different moment.

## 1. The event feed — announcements and updates

Post to the **event feed** for updates that registered riders and event followers
see on the event page: schedule notes, course changes, weather calls, results
recaps. Feed posts can also notify the event's audience, so they're good for
"everyone should know this" messages. You can target a post by registration
status or category when you only need to reach some riders.

Best for: pre-event information, day-of updates, post-event recaps.

## 2. Broadcasts — a direct push to everyone

A **broadcast** sends a notification (with an optional link) straight to **all
participants** in the event. It's the most direct way to get a short, important
message in front of riders quickly.

Best for: time-sensitive alerts — "neutral start delayed 15 minutes", "route
diverted at checkpoint 3".

## 3. SOS chat — talking to one rider in trouble

When a rider raises an **SOS**, you can **message them directly** from the alert,
and they can reply — a back-and-forth thread while you coordinate help. See
[Handling SOS alerts](handling-sos-alerts.md).

Best for: a live safety situation with a specific rider.

## Which to use

| You want to… | Use |
|---|---|
| Share info on the event page (and optionally notify) | **Event feed** |
| Push a short, urgent message to all riders now | **Broadcast** |
| Reach a subset (one category / status) | **Event feed**, targeted |
| Talk to one rider during an SOS | **SOS chat** |

## Good to know

- Riders control their own notification preferences, so non-urgent messages may
  not push to everyone — the in-app feed and notification list still capture them.
- Keep broadcasts for genuinely time-sensitive messages so riders keep paying
  attention to them.
- See the rider's side in [Notifications](../notifications/notification-types.md).
