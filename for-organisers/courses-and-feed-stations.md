# Courses and feed stations

If you run several races on the same roads — common at a festival — you can define
a **course** once and reuse it, instead of uploading a GPX for every race.

## What a course is

A course is a reusable template holding:

- The **route shape** (geometry).
- The **start and finish** lines and lap-detection settings.
- **Feed stations** along the route.

You build it once, then **link** it to as many events as you like.

## Reusing and updating

- **Link a course to an event** to apply its route and lines to that race.
- **Edit the course once and push the changes to every linked race** — so a
  last-minute reroute updates all your distances at once, rather than editing each
  event by hand.

This pairs naturally with [festivals](festivals.md), where several distances share
the same roads.

## Feed stations

Feed stations are named points along the course — aid stations, water stops, feed
zones — each with a location. They appear as markers on the map so riders and
spectators can see where support is. Add them to a course (so every linked race
gets them) or to an individual event.

## Good to know

- Editing a course and applying it updates all linked races, so double-check
  before pushing a change during an event weekend.
- For one-off events you don't need a course at all — just upload the GPX directly
  on the event. See [Routes, start and finish lines](routes-start-and-finish.md).
