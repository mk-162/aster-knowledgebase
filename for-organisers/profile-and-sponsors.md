# Your organiser profile, sponsors and audience report

## Your public organiser page

An **organiser account** has its own public page, separate from any club or team
page you run. It lives at your handle (in the app, open your organiser account and
share its page). On it you control:

- Your **name**, **logo** and **banner**.
- A **description**, your **website** and **social links**.
- A **verified** badge, when the Aster team grants one.

The page carries your branding and lets riders and fans find your events.
**Social links** aren't collected when you first create the account — you add them
here, by editing the organiser profile, once it exists.

## Followers

Riders and fans can **follow** your organiser account. When you publish a new
public or unlisted event, your followers can be notified — a simple way to bring
your audience back for each event. (Private events don't notify followers, by
design.)

## Sponsors

You can feature **sponsors** on an **event**, on your **organiser account**, or on
a **team** you run. Each sponsor has a name, logo and link, and they render as a
strip of circular logos on the relevant public page — a clean, consistent way to
give partners visibility. Add and reorder them from the sponsors screen for that
event, org or team.

- Sponsor links are safety-checked before they're shown.
- Keep your own logo and banner current — they're the first thing riders and
  spectators see.

## The audience report — proof for your sponsors

After (and during) an event, you can pull a printable **audience report** for it.
It rolls up how many people watched the live map, and it's built to hand to a
sponsor as evidence of reach. It shows:

- **Total watchers** — unique viewing sessions.
- **Peak concurrent** — the most people watching at once.
- **Watcher-minutes** — total attention time across the event.
- **Sponsor-slot impressions** — how many spectator sessions saw the event's
  sponsor slot on the live map and leaderboard.

The report carries **no personal data** — just aggregate audience counts — and
you can **print it or save it as a PDF**. It's **organiser-only**: only the people
who manage the event can open it.
