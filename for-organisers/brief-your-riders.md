# What to tell your riders before the event

Most tracking problems on the day come from riders who didn't set up beforehand.
Send your participants this short briefing (copy, paste and tweak) a few days out.

## Copy-and-send briefing

> **Getting set up for [event name]**
>
> We're tracking this event live with Aster. Please do all this **before** you
> arrive — ideally on Wi-Fi at home:
>
> 1. **Install the Aster app** (iPhone or Android). You need the app to *be
>    tracked*; the website is only for watching.
> 2. **Create an account** and sign in.
> 3. **Register for the event**, then **open it once while you still have signal**
>    — this downloads the route and map so you can start even with no signal on
>    the day.
> 4. **Allow location "all the time"** and **allow notifications** when the app
>    asks, so background tracking and alerts work.
> 5. **(Android)** Turn off battery optimisation for the app, so it isn't shut
>    down in your pocket.
> 6. **Arrive with a good charge** and pick a battery mode that suits the
>    distance.
>
> On the day: open the app, wait for the start window, let GPS warm up, and tap
> **Start**. Lost signal mid-ride? Keep riding — your track is saved on the phone
> and catches up when you reconnect. The app won't stop your ride or log you out
> on its own.

## Why each step matters

- **Open the event once with signal** is the big one — it caches the route so an
  offline start works with no bars on the day. See
  [What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).
- **Background location + battery settings** are what keep tracking alive with the
  phone in a pocket. See
  [Tips for a reliable track](../for-athletes/tips-for-a-reliable-track.md).
- **A good charge and the right battery mode** matter most on long days — point
  riders at [Battery modes](../for-athletes/battery-modes.md).

## A word on safety

Tell riders plainly what Aster's SOS does: it **alerts you, the organisers**, and
shares the rider's location. It **does not call the emergency services** — a rider
who needs help must call it themselves (their screen has a one-tap **Call emergency
services (999 / 112)** button). Brief your real emergency plan separately; the app
is an aid, not a rescue service. See
[Handling SOS alerts](handling-sos-alerts.md).

## On the day, from your side

Have your [race-day dashboard](race-day-management.md) open, start the gun on time
(or let Aster auto-start at the scheduled moment), watch for
[SOS alerts](handling-sos-alerts.md), and set a rider's status by hand if someone
crossed the line offline or pulled out. The full sequence is in the
[quick-start checklist](quick-start-checklist.md).
