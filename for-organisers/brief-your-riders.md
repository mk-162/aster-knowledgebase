# What to tell your riders before the event

Most race-day tracking problems come from riders who didn't set up beforehand.
Send your participants this short briefing (copy, paste and tweak) a few days out.

## Copy-and-send briefing

> **Getting set up for [event name]**
>
> We're tracking this event live with Watch the Dot. Please do this **before** you
> arrive — ideally on Wi-Fi:
>
> 1. **Install the Watch the Dot app** (iOS or Android). You need the app to be
>    tracked; the website is only for watching.
> 2. **Create an account** and sign in.
> 3. **Register for the event** and **open it once while you have signal** — this
>    downloads the route so you can start even with no signal on the day.
> 4. **Allow location "all the time"** and **allow notifications** when asked, so
>    background tracking and alerts work.
> 5. **(Android)** Exclude the app from battery optimisation so it isn't shut down
>    in your pocket.
> 6. **Add your emergency contact** in your profile.
> 7. **Arrive with a good charge** and pick a battery mode that suits the distance.
>
> On the day: open the app, wait for the start window, let GPS warm up, and tap
> **Start**. Lost signal mid-ride? Keep going — your track is saved and catches up
> when you reconnect. The app won't stop your ride or log you out on its own.

## Why each step matters

- **Open the event once with signal** is the big one — it caches the route so an
  offline start works. See
  [What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md).
- **Background location + battery exclusion** are what keep tracking alive with the
  phone in a pocket. See
  [Tips for a reliable track](../for-athletes/tips-for-a-reliable-track.md).
- **Emergency contact** means your safety net is in place. Remember Watch the Dot's
  SOS is an alert and location share, **not** a call to emergency services — brief
  your real safety plan separately.

## On the day, from your side

Have your [race-day dashboard](race-day-management.md) open, start the gun on time,
watch for [SOS alerts](handling-sos-alerts.md), and use **force finish / reset**
for any rider who needs a hand. The full sequence is in the
[quick-start checklist](quick-start-checklist.md).
