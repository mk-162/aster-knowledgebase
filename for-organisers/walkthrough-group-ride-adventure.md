# Walkthrough: a group ride or adventure

This format covers **social group rides** and **long-distance, point-to-point
adventures** (grand-départ style). Riders set off individually, it's usually
about taking part rather than racing, and the route often runs point to point
with waypoints along the way.

## 1. Create the event as a "group ride / adventure"

Choose the group-ride / adventure type when you
[create the event](create-an-event.md). Riders start individually rather than on
a shared gun.

## 2. Route and checkpoints

[Add your route](routes-start-and-finish.md). For a long point-to-point, set
separate **start** and **finish** lines. Then place **checkpoints** along the way
— aid stations, key turns, scenic markers. Checkpoints:

- Show up on the map for riders and spectators.
- Mark progress along a long route where a single finish line isn't enough.

## 3. Timing and cut-offs

These events are typically not about a winner, but you can still set a **cut-off**
if there's a hard course-closing time. Riders are timed from when they
individually start.

## 4. Registration

Set [registration](registration-and-categories.md) to **open** for a relaxed
sign-up, or **by approval** if you're vetting entries. Categories are optional —
you might run a single "all riders" group, or split by distance.

## 5. On the day

- Riders **start when they're ready** and are tracked the whole way.
- Spectators follow everyone's dots along the route and through the checkpoints.
- The same safety tools apply — riders have **SOS** and **crash detection**, and
  you see [SOS alerts](handling-sos-alerts.md) on your dashboard. For remote
  adventures, brief riders that the app keeps recording even with no signal and
  catches up later. [What happens when I lose signal?](../safety-and-offline/offline-and-no-signal.md)

## 6. After

Riders' rides are saved with full routes and stats. If your event has a
classification, [publish results](results.md); otherwise the value is the live
following and the saved adventures.

## Things to get right

- **Checkpoints carry a long route.** Place them at meaningful points so riders
  and spectators can track progress between start and finish.
- **Brief riders on offline behaviour.** On remote routes, reassure them the app
  keeps recording with no signal — their dot just catches up when they reconnect.
- **Lean on the safety tools.** For self-supported adventures, make sure riders
  know about SOS and have set emergency contacts.
