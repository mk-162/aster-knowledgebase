# Results

## Live leaderboard vs official results

During the event, the **leaderboard** is computed live from riders' GPS tracking.
It's the real-time picture of who's where.

**Official results** are what you publish at the end. Publishing ranks finishers
within each category and marks the results as official, so riders and spectators
see a settled, final classification rather than the live in-progress board.

## Publishing results

When the event is done, **publish results**. This:

- Ranks finishers within each category.
- Assigns each rider a category position.
- Marks the results official on the event's results page.

Only active registrations are included — withdrawn or cancelled entries are left
out.

## Importing timed results

If you use a chip-timing provider, results can be **imported** into the event
(matched to riders by bib number), including chip times, gun times, positions and
splits. Any bibs that don't match a registered rider are reported back so you can
fix them and re-import. Re-importing is safe — it updates rather than duplicates.

## Reading and exporting

The results page is public for public and unlisted events (private events require
access). Results can be **exported as a spreadsheet (CSV)** for your records or to
share with a federation.

## Good to know

- The live leaderboard is built from tracking; official results can also reflect
  chip times you import — handy when chip timing is the authority for placings.
- Because finished riders freeze at the line, late GPS data never changes a
  finisher's position after the fact.
