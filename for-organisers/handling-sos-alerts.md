# Handling SOS alerts

A rider in your event can raise an emergency **SOS** during their ride, and the
app can also raise one automatically if it detects a crash. In an event, an SOS
alerts **you, the organisers** — so your dashboard is where you see and manage
these alerts.

## What an SOS is — and is not

Be clear on this before your event, and brief your riders on it too.

- An SOS is an **in-app alert and location share**. It tells the organisers that
  a rider needs help and shows their last known position.
- Aster **does not contact the emergency services**. It cannot call 999 for a
  rider. The rider's own screen offers a one-tap **Call 999** button, but placing
  that call is down to the rider or to you — the app never does it.
- An SOS **does not guarantee anyone will respond**. It is an aid, not a rescue
  service and not a safety device. Your event still needs a real emergency plan.

Deciding whether and when to escalate to the emergency services is **your call**,
not the app's.

## What you see

Active SOS alerts appear on your [race-day dashboard](race-day-management.md),
highlighted so they're impossible to miss, and refreshed every few seconds. Each
alert shows:

- The **rider** who raised it.
- Their **last known location** so you can find them.
- When it was raised and its current **status**.

If the rider has chosen to share their phone number for emergencies, it appears
on the alert too — otherwise it stays hidden.

Resolved and past alerts move to a separate **SOS history** view, so the live
dashboard only shows what's still open.

## Responding

You move an alert through these states:

- **Responding** — you've seen it and you're dealing with it. This records that
  you're on it and who acknowledged it.
- **Resolved** — the situation is handled. This closes the alert.
- **False alarm** — not a real emergency (an accidental trigger, say). This also
  closes the alert.

You can **message the rider** directly from the alert. They receive your message
as a notification and can reply, giving you a two-way thread while you coordinate
help.

## Important: an SOS never ends the ride

Raising an SOS is completely separate from finishing. The rider keeps being
tracked — which is exactly what you want when someone may need help, so their dot
stays live and their location keeps updating. To end a rider's ride you set their
status yourself (see [Running the event on race day](race-day-management.md)); an
SOS on its own changes nothing about their race record.

Crash detection works the same way: if the app thinks a rider has crashed it
starts an SOS countdown, but it never stops the ride. A rider who's just hit the
ground may be the one who most needs their dot to stay visible.
