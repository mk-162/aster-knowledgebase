# Handling SOS alerts

Riders can raise an emergency **SOS** during a ride, and the app can also raise
one automatically if it detects a crash. As an organiser you see these alerts on
your dashboard so you can act.

## What you see

An SOS alert shows:

- The **rider** and their **live location** (with map link).
- When it was raised.
- Its current **status**.

Active alerts are highlighted prominently so they're impossible to miss, and the
dashboard keeps refreshing.

## Responding

You can move an alert through these states:

- **Responding** — you've seen it and are dealing with it (this acknowledges the
  alert and records who's on it).
- **Resolved** — the situation is handled; you can add resolution notes.
- **False alarm** — not a real emergency (for example an accidental trigger);
  this closes the alert.

You can also **message the rider** directly from the alert — they get it as a
notification and can reply, giving you a back-and-forth thread.

## Important

- **An SOS does not stop the rider's ride.** Raising the alarm is separate from
  finishing — the rider keeps being tracked, which is exactly what you want when
  someone may need help.
- SOS notifications reach the rider's watchers (and any emergency contacts they've
  set) as well as your dashboard.
- Aster's SOS is an **in-app alert and location share** — it is not a
  replacement for calling the emergency services. Make sure your event has a real
  emergency plan.
