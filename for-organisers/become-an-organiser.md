# Become an organiser

To create and run events you need an **organiser account** — the brand that hosts
your events (your club, race series or company). It's separate from your personal
profile, and more than one person can help manage it.

## Create an organiser account

1. From your profile, choose to create an organiser account.
2. Give it a **name**, an optional **@handle**, a **logo**, and a short
   **description**.
3. You're now an admin of that organiser account and can create events under it.

You can be an admin of more than one organiser account (for example if you run
several series).

## Your public organiser page

Each organiser account gets a public page at its handle (`…/orgs/@yourhandle`)
showing your logo, description, website and social links, your **verified** badge
(if you have one), and all your public and unlisted events. Riders can **follow**
your organiser account to be notified when you publish new events. See
[Your organiser profile and sponsors](profile-and-sponsors.md).

## Verification

A **verified** badge signals to riders that an organiser is the genuine article.
Verification is granted by the Watch the Dot team. If you'd like to be verified,
get in touch — you may be asked for a few details about your organisation and
your events.

## Who can manage your events

Anyone you add as an **admin** of the organiser account can create and manage its
events. Individual events can also have their own admins. This lets you share
race-day duties across a team without sharing a single login.

## Good to know

- Creating an organiser account is free.
- The hosting organiser (or team) is chosen when you create an event and can't be
  changed afterwards — pick the right one at the start.
