# Become an organiser

To create and run events you need a **host** — the brand that puts on your events.
That's usually an **organiser account** (your club, race series or company), and
it's separate from your personal profile so more than one person can help run it.

Anyone can create one — there's no application or approval step.

## Create an organiser account

1. From your profile, choose to **Create organiser account**.
2. Give it a **name** and a **@handle** (the app checks the handle is free as you
   type).
3. Add the optional extras: a **logo**, a **banner**, a **description** and a
   **website**. (The create form is deliberately light — **social links** are
   added later when you edit the organiser profile. See
   [Your organiser profile and sponsors](profile-and-sponsors.md).)
4. You're now the owner of that account and can create events under it.

You can own or admin more than one organiser account — for example if you run
several separate series.

## Hosting events from a team

A **team or club** can also host events. If you run a club on Aster, you can create
members-only rides and events under it directly — no separate organiser account
needed. See [Teams and clubs](../teams-and-clubs/index.md).

You pick the host (organiser account **or** team) when you create an event, and it
**can't be changed afterwards** — pick the right one at the start.

## Your public organiser page

Each organiser account gets a public page at its handle (`…/orgs/@yourhandle`)
showing your logo, description, website and social links, your **verified** badge
(if you have one), and your public and unlisted events. People can **follow** your
organiser account to be notified when you publish new events. See
[Your organiser profile and sponsors](profile-and-sponsors.md).

## Verification

A **verified** badge signals to athletes that an organiser is the genuine article.
Verification is granted by the Aster team — anyone can create an account, but the
badge is added by us. If you'd like to be verified, get in touch; you may be asked
for a few details about your organisation and your events.

## Who can manage your events

Anyone you add as an **admin** of the organiser account can create and manage its
events. Individual events can also have their own admins. This lets you share
race-day duties across a team without sharing one login.

## Good to know

- Creating an organiser account is **free**.
- Only the owner can add or remove other admins.
