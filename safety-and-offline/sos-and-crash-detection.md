# SOS and crash detection

Aster has two ways to raise the alarm if something goes wrong on a ride:
a manual **SOS** button, and automatic **crash detection**.

## Manual SOS

While tracking an event or a free ride, tap the red **SOS** button. To prevent
accidental pocket-taps, you'll confirm with a short code. Once confirmed:

- Your current **location** is captured and shared.
- An alert goes to the people watching your ride, to your **emergency contacts**
  (if you've set any), and — in an event — to the **organiser's dashboard**.
- Watchers and organisers can see where you are and respond.

**An SOS does not end your ride.** You stay tracked, which is exactly what you
want if you might need help — the people coming to assist can keep seeing your
position. You can carry on, or stop, whenever you're ready.

There's also an **I'm OK** button to reassure your watchers if you've been
stationary and people might be worried.

## Automatic crash detection

On the mobile app, Aster watches for the sudden deceleration that looks
like a crash. If it detects one:

1. An **alarm sounds** and your phone vibrates.
2. A **countdown** appears.
3. If you don't cancel it in time, an SOS is raised automatically with your
   location — just like a manual SOS.

If it was a false alarm (a kerb, a dropped phone), just cancel the countdown and
carry on.

## Who gets the alert

- **Your watchers** — anyone following your ride.
- **Your emergency contacts** — if you've set them on your profile.
- **The organiser** — for events, on their race-day dashboard. See
  [Handling SOS alerts](../for-organisers/handling-sos-alerts.md).

## Important

- SOS alerts **always get through** as notifications — they're never held back by
  a recipient's quiet hours. Safety comes first.
- Crash detection needs the phone's motion sensors, so it's a **mobile-app**
  feature.
- Aster's SOS is an **in-app alert and live location share** — it does
  **not** call the emergency services. Always carry a way to call for real help,
  and follow your event's safety briefing.
