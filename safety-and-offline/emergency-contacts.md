# Emergency contacts

Your **emergency contacts** are the people you want alerted if something goes
wrong on a ride.

## What they're for

If you raise an **SOS** — or automatic [crash detection](sos-and-crash-detection.md)
raises one for you — your emergency contacts are notified along with your live
location, so the people who matter most know where you are and that you need help.

## Setting them up

Add your emergency contacts in your profile settings before you ride. Keep them
current, especially before a big or remote event.

## Events that require one

Some organisers ask for an **emergency contact** as part of registering for their
event. If you see that field on the join form, it's required for that event — fill
it in so the organiser has it on the day. See
[Registration and categories](../for-organisers/registration-and-categories.md).

## Important

- Emergency contacts are used **only** for safety alerts — they're not shared
  publicly or used for anything else.
- Watch the Dot's SOS is an **in-app alert and location share**. It is **not** a
  call to the emergency services. Always carry a way to call for real help, and
  make sure someone knows your plans for remote rides.
