# Who can see your location?

This is the question that matters most with live tracking, so here's a clear,
honest answer.

## The short version

- Watch the Dot only records and shares your location **while you're actively
  tracking a ride** — not the rest of the time.
- **In an event,** your live position is visible to whoever can view that event.
- **On a free ride,** you're private by default — only people you send the link to
  can watch.
- When you **stop** or **finish**, the live sharing ends.

## While you're tracking

### Events

When you ride an event, being on the map is the point — spectators follow the
race. **Who** can see you depends on the event's privacy level:

| Event privacy | Who can see your dot |
|---|---|
| **Public** | Anyone — it's listed and openly watchable |
| **Unlisted** | Anyone who has the event link |
| **Private** | Only signed-in people with the organiser's access code |

So before you join, know the event's privacy level if that matters to you. See
[Public, unlisted and private events](../for-spectators/public-unlisted-private-events.md).

### Free rides

A free ride is **private by default**. Nobody can see it unless **you** create a
**watch link** and share it. Anyone you give that link to can watch you live while
the ride is on; people without it can't find your ride. See
[Free rides](../for-athletes/free-rides.md) and
[Share links](../for-spectators/share-links.md).

## When you're not riding

The app doesn't track you when you're not on a ride. Your location is only
recorded between the moment you **start** and the moment you **finish** — points
outside that window are never stored or shown. See
[Why your distance and time are accurate](../safety-and-offline/data-accuracy.md).

## Your saved rides

After a ride, what stays visible depends on its visibility:

- A **public** ride appears on your profile for anyone to see.
- A **private** ride is visible only to you (and anyone who still has a share
  link you sent).

## Emergency contacts

Your [emergency contacts](../safety-and-offline/emergency-contacts.md) receive
your location **only if you raise an SOS** (or crash detection does). They're not
given any ongoing access to where you are.

## How to keep a ride private

- **Use a free ride** (private by default) rather than a public event if you don't
  want an audience.
- **Don't share the watch link** — without it, nobody can find a free ride.
- **Check an event's privacy level** before you register.
- Manage your profile and ride visibility in your settings — see
  [Your profile and handle](your-profile-and-handle.md).
