# Export or delete your data

Both live in the app under **Settings → Privacy & data**. You'll need to be
signed in.

## Export your data

Tap **Download my data** to get a complete copy of what Aster holds for
you, as a single JSON file. It includes your profile, the events you took
part in and your free rides, all your GPS points, your messages, follows,
notifications and registered devices. It's the full record — your own copy
to keep or take elsewhere.

On a phone, the app hands the file to your device's share sheet so you can
save it wherever you like. On the web, it downloads to your browser. Export
is rate-limited (a few requests a minute), so if you tap it repeatedly,
wait a moment and try again.

## Delete your account

Tap **Delete account** to permanently erase your account. This is
**irreversible**:

- Your profile, rides, GPS history, posts and follows are removed.
- Anything you posted that others might still see in a thread — a message,
  a comment — has its text replaced with **[deleted]** and is detached from
  you.
- Your sign-in identity is removed, so you can no longer log in.

Some records with a lawful reason to be kept — for example a payment
receipt — are retained with your identity stripped out.

### The safety checks

Two things stop this happening by accident:

- You **can't delete while a ride is in progress**. Stop or finish your
  ride first, so live tracking data isn't destroyed mid-ride.
- You have to **confirm by typing** a short confirmation word before the
  delete goes through.

## Good to know

- You gave consent to the privacy policy when you signed up, and you can
  review it and manage optional marketing emails under **Settings →
  Privacy & data**.
- If you'd rather step back than delete, you can unfollow accounts, mute
  notifications, or make rides private instead. See
  [Privacy and your data](privacy-and-your-data.md).
