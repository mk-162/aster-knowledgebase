# Privacy and your data

Aster only knows where you are when you choose to share it. This page says
what's held about you and why, and points you to the controls.

## What's held about you

- **Account basics.** Your email and the profile details you add — name,
  photo, banner, bio, location, social links.
- **Location — only during a ride you start.** When you tap Start, the app
  records GPS points for that ride. Nothing is recorded before you start or
  after you stop.
- **Rides and events.** The events you register for, your routes,
  distances, times, and any posts or comments you publish.
- **Activity.** Who you follow, your notifications, and the devices
  registered to receive push alerts.
- **The minimum technical data** needed to keep the service running. Aster
  doesn't sell your data or build advertising profiles from it.

## How visibility works

- Watching **public and unlisted** events needs **no account**, so
  spectators aren't asked for data they don't need.
- Your **email and password** are never shown publicly.
- **Free rides** are private by default — visible only to you and anyone
  you give a watch link.
- GPS points outside a ride's start–finish window are never stored or
  shown to anyone.

When you join an event or start a shared ride where your live position
will be public, Aster records that you understood and agreed to it — a
one-off acknowledgement at that moment, not a setting you have to hunt for.

For the full picture of who sees your position and when, see
[Who can see your location?](who-can-see-your-location.md).

## You stay signed in

Aster never signs you out because of a network or server error — not
mid-ride, not in a dead zone. The only things that end your session are
signing out yourself, or the app detecting its saved login is corrupted on
your device. If your login needs refreshing, a small banner asks you to
sign in again; your ride and data stay safe on the device in the meantime.
See [Account and signing in](account-and-signing-in.md).

## You're in control

- Edit or remove your public details any time — see
  [Your profile and handle](your-profile-and-handle.md).
- Choose what you're notified about — see
  [Preferences and quiet hours](../notifications/preferences-and-quiet-hours.md).
- Manage optional marketing emails, **export** everything you've stored, or
  **delete** your account entirely — all under **Settings → Privacy &
  data**. See [Export or delete your data](export-or-delete-your-data.md).

## Good to know

- An **SOS** shares your location with the people watching your ride so
  they can help — it doesn't contact the emergency services. See
  [SOS and crash detection](../safety-and-offline/sos-and-crash-detection.md).
